Comprehensive Overview

QuickSecure is a rapid-response security and lockdown system designed specifically for schools. In moments of crisis—such as an active shooter or other high-threat events—QuickSecure acts decisively, locking down selected classrooms and areas to safeguard students and staff. Unlike traditional locks that may take valuable time to engage, QuickSecure can secure multiple doors instantly, triggered either manually or electronically.

Through a combination of integrated sensors, digital floor plans, and scheduling data, QuickSecure intelligently determines which rooms should remain locked or accessible, ensuring that anyone caught in hallways can quickly find safe shelter. This data-driven approach also provides real-time information to first responders and administrators via a secure on-site web server. They immediately gain insights into the building’s layout, current lockdown status, and potential threat locations. This empowers first responders to coordinate their actions more effectively, ultimately reducing response times and saving lives.

Electronically Assisted QuickSecure

The Electronically Assisted QuickSecure integrates cutting-edge technology for remote and centralized control:

  • DC Motor: Instantly locks doors at the press of a button.
  • Electronic Control System: Connects to the building’s central unit, providing real-time status monitoring and ML-driven analytics.

This model ensures immediate responses to threats, allowing administrators to secure multiple rooms simultaneously.

Manual QuickSecure

The Manual QuickSecure offers a straightforward, mechanically operated solution:

  • Rail System: Adapts to all standard door frames for optimal positioning.
  • Slide Lock: A simple, robust manual locking mechanism.
  • Status Indicators: Visual cues for immediate confirmation of lock status.
  • High-Strength Materials: Carbon fiber and PLA ensure durability and lightweight handling.

A cost-effective option for enhancing security without electronic infrastructure.

Data-Driven Decision Making

QuickSecure leverages advanced data-driven decision-making to deliver a robust solution that arms first responders and administrators with precise, real-time information.

  • Floor Plan & Schedule Integration: QuickSecure analyzes digital floor plans, classroom schedules, and bell timetables. Rooms with no scheduled occupants remain unlocked initially, allowing students or educators in hallways to quickly find refuge.
  • Sound Source Localization: By detecting and triangulating sounds such as gunshots, QuickSecure uses time differences of sound arrival to accurately map the location of an active threat.
  • Automated Lockdown Strategy: The system processes environmental data to determine which rooms to lock or leave accessible, optimizing safety and ensuring that the right areas are secured at the right time.
  • Web Server Integration: All this data is presented on an on-site hosted web server, providing administrators and first responders with an up-to-the-minute visual map. This allows for a swift and informed response, guiding them to the source of the threat and indicating safe zones.

By synthesizing environmental cues, schedules, and acoustic intelligence, QuickSecure enables responders and officials to make rapid, evidence-based decisions under pressure, ultimately enhancing safety and security.

Why This Is Needed

In recent years, active shooter incidents in schools have continued to increase. According to various studies and reports (e.g., the FBI’s active shooter reports), these tragic events have nearly doubled over the last two decades. This reality demands proactive measures that can swiftly secure classrooms, minimize exposure to danger, and ultimately save lives.

Beyond the immediate physical threat, prolonged exposure to such high-stress situations can cause lasting emotional trauma for students and educators. Research shows that even short incidents can lead to anxiety, stress disorders, and long-term mental health challenges. By drastically reducing the time occupants remain in harm’s way, QuickSecure not only helps ensure their physical safety but also decreases the psychological toll. The faster a threat is contained, the sooner educators can tend to their students’ emotional needs, help them regain a sense of security, and begin the healing process.

QuickSecure takes human hesitation and complex decision-making out of the equation. In a matter of moments, it intelligently locks down the necessary areas, allowing teachers and staff to focus on guiding students and coordinating safe egress. With gun-related threats showing no signs of disappearing, preparation and rapid response are paramount. QuickSecure’s data-driven approach ensures that schools are equipped to face these challenges head-on, fostering an environment where learning, recovery, and resilience are possible, even in the face of emerging threats.

  • Enhancing on-site security preparedness through instantaneous lockdown.
  • Reducing response times to limit both physical exposure and emotional harm.
  • Empowering educators and staff to concentrate on student well-being rather than struggling with mechanical locks.
  • Providing a proactive, intelligent strategy that safeguards lives and aids quicker post-incident recovery.

Our Story

QuickSecure began as an idea born out of necessity. Founded by Rohan Kumar and William Susskind in 11th grade, the company was created in response to a staggering and heartbreaking reality: the number of active gun threats in schools surpassed the days elapsed in the year. This alarming statistic drove us to take action, to innovate, and to seek a solution that could truly make a difference.

When we looked at the existing market, we were disheartened. Many solutions lacked fire safety compliance, making them unsuitable for U.S. public schools. Others placed students in greater danger or simply attempted to mitigate the problem without effectively addressing it. We knew we had to create something better—something that not only complied with safety regulations but actively protected students and educators in life-threatening situations.

With determination and guidance from our engineering teachers, we developed the idea for Quick Secure. Encouraged by our educators, we entered our concept into several competitions, and the response was overwhelming. We were honored to win the K-12 InVenture Prize at Georgia Institute of Technology, which propelled us to the National Invention Convention. There, we were awarded the prestigious WilmerHale Patent Award, which allowed us to secure a patent for our product and bring it closer to market.

Along the way, we’ve partnered with organizations such as Ryerson Steel and Siemens Electrical, as well as other companies that believed in our mission. These collaborations have enabled us to refine and advance QuickSecure into the reliable, innovative system it is today.

As students ourselves, our mission remains deeply personal: to protect our peers, educators, and communities from the tragic violence that has become all too common. We are committed to making QuickSecure accessible to those who need it most, providing a solution that prioritizes safety, compliance, and peace of mind.
