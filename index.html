Comprehensive Overview

QuickSecure is a school safety operating system built to secure campuses during active threats through smart locks, AI-powered gunshot detection, and a real-time dashboard. In emergencies, staff can trigger instant lockdowns through mobile panic buttons or centralized controls, securing targeted rooms across the building in seconds. The system is fully retrofit and code compliant, designed to work with existing infrastructure without requiring major renovations.

Using live floor plan visualization, schedule integration, and environmental sensor inputs, QuickSecure determines which doors to lock and which should remain accessible to help those in transition find safe shelter. Every decision is reflected instantly on a local dashboard shared with administrators and first responders, providing live insight into door status, threat location, and campus safety conditions. By streamlining fragmented systems into a single, intelligent response platform, QuickSecure reduces chaos, improves clarity, and helps protect lives when every second counts.

Electronically Assisted QuickSecure

The Electronically Assisted QuickSecure integrates cutting-edge technology for remote and centralized control:

  • DC Motor: Instantly locks doors at the press of a button.
  • Electronic Control System: Connects to the building’s central unit, providing real-time status monitoring and ML-driven analytics.

This model ensures immediate responses to threats, allowing administrators to secure multiple rooms simultaneously.

Manual QuickSecure

The Manual QuickSecure offers a straightforward, mechanically operated solution:

  • Rail System: Adapts to all standard door frames for optimal positioning.
  • Slide Lock: A simple, robust manual locking mechanism.
  • Status Indicators: Visual cues for immediate confirmation of lock status.
  • High-Strength Materials: Carbon fiber and PLA ensure durability and lightweight handling.

A cost-effective option for enhancing security without electronic infrastructure.

Unified School Safety Dashboard

QuickSecure is a complete School Safety Operating System that brings all safety information into one simple dashboard. It helps schools comply with Georgia HB 268 (Ricky and Alissa’s Law) and gives both staff and first responders the real-time visibility they need during emergencies.

  • Live Floor Plan Visualization: View door status, room occupancy, and active alerts across campus on one clear map. Know what is happening and where, instantly.
  • Mobile Panic Alerts: Staff can trigger an instant lockdown from their device, alerting responders and updating the system in real time.
  • AI-Based Gunshot Detection: QuickSecure identifies and locates gunfire using acoustic triangulation and displays the threat location immediately.
  • Adaptive Lockdown Strategy: The system references schedules, floor plans, and sensor inputs to decide which rooms to secure and which to leave accessible for safety.

By integrating detection, communication, and visibility, QuickSecure allows schools and responders to act faster and smarter—making the entire campus safer for everyone inside.

Why This Is Needed

In recent years, school shootings have sharply increased, with more than 350 incidents reported in 2023 alone. These events are not only becoming more frequent, but also more deadly and disruptive. This escalation demands more than fragmented tools or reactive strategies—it calls for integrated systems that proactively secure buildings and guide emergency response in real time.

The impact of school shootings goes far beyond the moment of crisis. Students exposed to gun violence show measurable declines in academic performance, college enrollment, and long-term earnings. Nearly two out of three students now report living in constant fear of a school shooting, and schools are struggling to restore both physical safety and emotional security.

QuickSecure was built to meet this need:

  • Fully aligned with Georgia House Bill 268 (Ricky and Alassay’s Law), which mandates digital floorplans, mobile panic alerts, and first responder integration by July 1, 2026.
  • Uses AI-powered gunshot detection and real-time sound localization to track threats as they happen.
  • Displays a live floorplan dashboard, giving responders immediate clarity on lockdown status and threat locations.
  • Locks down buildings intelligently, allowing staff to focus on students rather than mechanical procedures.

The faster a threat is contained, the faster the recovery begins. QuickSecure helps ensure that both safety and healing can start the moment a crisis unfolds.

Our Story

QuickSecure began as an idea born out of necessity. Founded by Rohan Kumar and William Susskind in 11th grade, the company was created in response to a staggering and heartbreaking reality: the number of active gun threats in schools surpassed the days elapsed in the year. This alarming statistic drove us to take action, to innovate, and to seek a solution that could truly make a difference.

When we looked at the existing market, we were disheartened. Many solutions lacked fire safety compliance, making them unsuitable for U.S. public schools. Others placed students in greater danger or simply attempted to mitigate the problem without effectively addressing it. We knew we had to create something better—something that not only complied with safety regulations but actively protected students and educators in life-threatening situations.

With determination and guidance from our engineering teachers, we developed the idea for Quick Secure. Encouraged by our educators, we entered our concept into several competitions, and the response was overwhelming. We were honored to win the K-12 InVenture Prize at Georgia Institute of Technology, which propelled us to the National Invention Convention. There, we were awarded the prestigious WilmerHale Patent Award, which allowed us to secure a patent for our product and bring it closer to market.

Along the way, we’ve partnered with organizations such as Ryerson Steel and Siemens Electrical, as well as other companies that believed in our mission. These collaborations have enabled us to refine and advance QuickSecure into the reliable, innovative system it is today.

As students ourselves, our mission remains deeply personal: to protect our peers, educators, and communities from the tragic violence that has become all too common. We are committed to making QuickSecure accessible to those who need it most, providing a solution that prioritizes safety, compliance, and peace of mind.

Installing the QuickSecure is a seamless process. Here's what you can expect:

  • Professional Assessment: A certified Quick Secure technician will visit your location to assess and measure all doors where the installation is intended. This ensures a precise fit and optimal security.
  • Material Approval: Based on the assessment, the technician will recommend the appropriate materials for your doors. Once approved by you, the installation process begins.
  • Precise Installation: Small drill holes will be carefully placed in strategic positions on the door. The QuickSecure will then be securely screwed into these holes, creating a strong and reliable attachment.
